Market

TAM, SAM, and SOM

Three circles that help you understand the size of an opportunity.

Market sizing can sound like investor theater, but the idea is simple: how many people could need this, how many can you realistically reach, and how much of that group might choose you first?

The three circles

TAM means total addressable market. It is the biggest circle: everyone who could need this kind of product or service.

SAM means serviceable available market. It is the part of the market you can actually serve based on location, price, language, delivery, regulations, or capacity.

SOM means serviceable obtainable market. It is the slice you can reasonably win in the near term.

For a home baker in Austin, TAM might be people who buy baked goods. SAM might be Austin-area customers who buy custom cookies for events. SOM might be ten birthday-party customers per month reached through school groups, referrals, and Instagram.

Try this: Do not chase the biggest number. A small, reachable market is usually more useful than a huge market you cannot talk to.

Use TAM, SAM, and SOM to make calmer decisions. If your first group is small but excited, that is not a failure. It is often the fastest way to learn what should come next.
