Brainstorming 101

A calmer way to move ideas from your head onto the page.

Brainstorming works best when you separate two jobs: making ideas and judging ideas. Most people try to do both at once, which is why the page stays blank.

Start with quantity

Set a timer for ten minutes and write every possible business idea, product name, customer, bundle, or service you can imagine. Do not stop to make it elegant. Messy counts.

Next, circle the ideas that have energy. Energy can mean you feel curious, someone has asked for it before, it solves a real problem, or you could test it without spending much money.

Use better prompts

  • What do people already ask me to make, fix, explain, or choose?
  • What would I still do if only ten people cared?
  • What could I sell in one afternoon as a tiny test?
  • What skill do I underestimate because it feels easy to me?
Try this: Pick one circled idea and write the smallest sellable version. Not the dream version. The smallest version someone could understand and say yes to.

Good brainstorming is not about finding the perfect idea. It is about creating enough options that the next step becomes visible.
