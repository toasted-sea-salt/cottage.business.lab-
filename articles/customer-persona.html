What is a Customer Persona?

A persona is a simple portrait of the kind of person you are trying to serve.

A customer persona is not a fake person you invent to sound official. It is a useful sketch of the people most likely to care about what you make.

For example, if you knit baby blankets, your persona might not be "everyone with a baby." It might be a grandmother who wants a meaningful shower gift, or a parent who values handmade items and is willing to wait two weeks for a custom color.

What to include

Keep it plain. Write down what this person is trying to do, what makes that hard, where they already shop or ask for advice, and what would make them trust you.

  • What problem, wish, or moment brings them to you?
  • What would make them hesitate?
  • What words do they use to describe the thing they want?
  • Where could you realistically reach them?
Try this: Interview three real people before polishing the persona. Listen for repeated phrases. Those phrases often become your best website copy.

A good persona helps you stop designing for "everybody." It gives your name, photos, prices, and offers a clearer direction. You can always make another persona later, but one honest starting point is enough.
